Punishment – An Introspective Didactic

What is punishment? Such a question may sound silly as this word allows memories, or stories from a vast spectrum of personal and impersonal experience. Though one must explore the concept of punishment to understand how vast this spectrum truly is. Punishment’s vastness is primarily due to a broad range of implications relative to the conditions leading to punishment’s being. Looking at definitions of punishment will showcase the extent to which one can explore possible ramifications of punishment. Punishment is defined as “the infliction or imposition of a penalty as retribution.” (Webster's) Punishment is also known as “rough treatment or handling inflicted on or suffered by a person or thing.” (Webster's) This contrast of meaning is worthy of pause. To truly understand punishment is to define retribution - or at very least recognize variations of what retribution means exist...
