Technology and Values, an Intersectional.

The value of technology can be both positive and negative; how and why technology is developed is at times just as important if not more so than what is developed. Technology shapes the world today through calculated ways to a largely incalculable extent. Calculated is development of the internet – a networking of computers allowing sharing of information. This networking is calculable solely to the extent that input corresponds to expected output. Networking of individual systems then expanded to what is known today colloquially as the internet, really refers to the World Wide Web or WWW. Incalculable are the numerous developments resultant from “the web” developing. Currently is believed about fifty percent of the global population has access to and/or uses the internet daily. Fifty percent of people able to exhaustively: research, communicate, and share! None of this would be if not for mathematics. Mathematics developed with tools like an abacus; for calculating, are the beginnings of what would be “the web” today. Building upon inventions of the past were people such as Charles Babbage. Charles Babbage developed mechanical computers with intentions like an abacus. Babbage’s abacus was namely a difference engine and an analytical engine for solving equations. These were not engines thought of today generating propulsion for cars. These engines were locomotives of mathematics, generating calculations like an abacus provided at faster rate. Computing is necessary as all internet and world wide web is computation based. Computations of data are what allow information to be seen and displayed. Displays are often demarked with a one or zero; known as binary notation. Data, all data: Documents typed with word are stored and quickly accessible because of computation. Understanding data’s facets allows understanding operations of the world wide web. Much like a word document. the world wide web is documents of varying types and specificity. Improvements such as Babbage’s are necessary relative to scale. Exemplary of need for scaling improvements is Herman Hollerith’s Desk. Scale is primary to understand innovation of “the web” as the web is symbolic of human growth much like the television. Television was able to connect people of vastly different locales to see: media, movies, experiences much like the web today. This parallel most demonstrable through online distributors such as Netflix. Herman Hollerith’s machine acted with interests of allowing seeing similarly like a television; distinction being a seeking for vast amounts of data input per its output rather than output of less measured input such as television offered. Explicitly Hollerith’s Desk invented during the 1800’s was to manage or observe population growth known as census accounting. This required much more speed as prior to this invention the United States Census required two years to tabulate. Hollerith’s Desk was such an improvement that the census of the 1880’s was completed within six weeks. When thinking of the World Wide Web is better to think of it as a hybrid of input and output, unlike television, WWW is more often immediately interactive. This historical breakthrough of computation parallels changes today.
